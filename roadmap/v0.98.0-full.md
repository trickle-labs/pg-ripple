## v0.98.0 — SKOS Support, Named Bundle API & Graph Intelligence

**Theme**: Complete SKOS/SKOS-XL entailment, formal named bundle API, contradiction explanation, federation trust scoring, and graph coverage metrics.

> **In plain language:** This release addresses five capabilities identified during riverbank integration planning — all of which are pure graph computation, useful independently of any application layer, and belong in pg_ripple rather than above it. The headline item is a complete W3C-conformant SKOS rule set. But the release also introduces a formal named bundle API so downstream tools can reference rule sets by stable, versioned names; a "why is this inconsistent?" explainer that traces the minimal set of facts causing a contradiction; a trust-scoring layer for data fetched from remote SPARQL endpoints; and a coverage dashboard that tells you how thoroughly a topic area is populated in the graph.
>
> **Effort estimate: 5–7 person-weeks**

---

### Background: What SKOS Defines

The [W3C SKOS Reference](https://www.w3.org/TR/skos-reference/) (Recommendation, 18 August 2009) defines 38 formal statements (S1–S46 plus SKOS-XL S47–S62) about SKOS properties and classes. These break into three categories relevant to pg_ripple:

1. **Entailments** — inferences that must fire when SKOS data is present (e.g., `skos:narrower` is the inverse of `skos:broader`; `skos:broaderTransitive` is the transitive closure of `skos:broader`; `skos:related` is symmetric).
2. **Integrity conditions** — constraints on well-formed SKOS data (e.g., at most one `skos:prefLabel` per language tag; `skos:related` and `skos:broaderTransitive` are disjoint).
3. **Sub-property hierarchies** — label and documentation properties inherit from `rdfs:label` and `skos:note` respectively, and mapping properties propagate into semantic relation properties.

pg_ripple's Datalog engine can express all of these directly as either inference rules or SHACL-style constraint rules. No special reasoning algorithms are needed beyond what is already implemented.

### Deliverables

#### SKOS-01 — `"skos"` Built-in Rule Set (28 rules)

Add a `"skos"` branch to `src/datalog/builtins.rs` covering all W3C SKOS entailment rules:

**Hierarchy rules (S22, S24, S25, S26)**

```datalog
# S22: skos:broader is a sub-property of skos:broaderTransitive
?x skos:broaderTransitive ?y :- ?x skos:broader ?y .

# S22: skos:narrower is a sub-property of skos:narrowerTransitive
?x skos:narrowerTransitive ?y :- ?x skos:narrower ?y .

# S24: skos:broaderTransitive is transitive
?x skos:broaderTransitive ?z :- ?x skos:broaderTransitive ?y, ?y skos:broaderTransitive ?z .

# S24: skos:narrowerTransitive is transitive
?x skos:narrowerTransitive ?z :- ?x skos:narrowerTransitive ?y, ?y skos:narrowerTransitive ?z .

# S25: skos:narrower is owl:inverseOf skos:broader
?y skos:narrower ?x :- ?x skos:broader ?y .
?y skos:broader  ?x :- ?x skos:narrower ?y .

# S26: skos:narrowerTransitive is owl:inverseOf skos:broaderTransitive
?y skos:narrowerTransitive ?x :- ?x skos:broaderTransitive ?y .
```

**Associative relation rules (S21, S23)**

```datalog
# S23: skos:related is symmetric
?y skos:related ?x :- ?x skos:related ?y .

# S21: skos:related is a sub-property of skos:semanticRelation
?x skos:semanticRelation ?y :- ?x skos:related ?y .

# S21: skos:broaderTransitive is a sub-property of skos:semanticRelation
?x skos:semanticRelation ?y :- ?x skos:broaderTransitive ?y .

# S21: skos:narrowerTransitive is a sub-property of skos:semanticRelation
?x skos:semanticRelation ?y :- ?x skos:narrowerTransitive ?y .
```

**Concept type inference (S19, S20)**

```datalog
# S19/S20: domain and range of skos:semanticRelation is skos:Concept
?x rdf:type skos:Concept :- ?x skos:semanticRelation ?y .
?y rdf:type skos:Concept :- ?x skos:semanticRelation ?y .
```

**Concept scheme rules (S7, S8, S4, S5, S6)**

```datalog
# S7: skos:topConceptOf is a sub-property of skos:inScheme
?x skos:inScheme ?s :- ?x skos:topConceptOf ?s .

# S8: skos:topConceptOf is owl:inverseOf skos:hasTopConcept
?x skos:topConceptOf ?s :- ?s skos:hasTopConcept ?x .
?s skos:hasTopConcept ?x :- ?x skos:topConceptOf ?s .

# S4: rdfs:range of skos:inScheme is skos:ConceptScheme
?s rdf:type skos:ConceptScheme :- ?x skos:inScheme ?s .

# S5/S6: domain/range of skos:hasTopConcept
?s rdf:type skos:ConceptScheme :- ?s skos:hasTopConcept ?x .
?x rdf:type skos:Concept       :- ?s skos:hasTopConcept ?x .
```

**Label rules (S11)**

```datalog
# S11: skos:prefLabel/altLabel/hiddenLabel are sub-properties of rdfs:label
?x rdfs:label ?l :- ?x skos:prefLabel   ?l .
?x rdfs:label ?l :- ?x skos:altLabel    ?l .
?x rdfs:label ?l :- ?x skos:hiddenLabel ?l .
```

**Documentation sub-property rules (S17)**

```datalog
# S17: documentation properties are sub-properties of skos:note
?x skos:note ?n :- ?x skos:changeNote    ?n .
?x skos:note ?n :- ?x skos:definition    ?n .
?x skos:note ?n :- ?x skos:editorialNote ?n .
?x skos:note ?n :- ?x skos:example       ?n .
?x skos:note ?n :- ?x skos:historyNote   ?n .
?x skos:note ?n :- ?x skos:scopeNote     ?n .
```

**Mapping property rules (S39–S45)**

```datalog
# S39: skos:mappingRelation is a sub-property of skos:semanticRelation
?x skos:semanticRelation ?y :- ?x skos:mappingRelation ?y .

# S40: closeMatch/broadMatch/narrowMatch/relatedMatch sub-properties of mappingRelation
?x skos:mappingRelation ?y :- ?x skos:closeMatch    ?y .
?x skos:mappingRelation ?y :- ?x skos:broadMatch    ?y .
?x skos:mappingRelation ?y :- ?x skos:narrowMatch   ?y .
?x skos:mappingRelation ?y :- ?x skos:relatedMatch  ?y .

# S41: broadMatch/narrowMatch/relatedMatch propagate into hierarchy/associative
?x skos:broader ?y :- ?x skos:broadMatch   ?y .
?x skos:narrower ?y :- ?x skos:narrowMatch  ?y .
?x skos:related  ?y :- ?x skos:relatedMatch ?y .

# S42: exactMatch is a sub-property of closeMatch
?x skos:closeMatch ?y :- ?x skos:exactMatch ?y .

# S43: skos:narrowMatch is owl:inverseOf skos:broadMatch
?y skos:narrowMatch ?x :- ?x skos:broadMatch  ?y .
?y skos:broadMatch  ?x :- ?x skos:narrowMatch ?y .

# S44: closeMatch/relatedMatch/exactMatch are symmetric
?y skos:closeMatch   ?x :- ?x skos:closeMatch   ?y .
?y skos:relatedMatch ?x :- ?x skos:relatedMatch ?y .
?y skos:exactMatch   ?x :- ?x skos:exactMatch   ?y .

# S45: exactMatch is transitive
?x skos:exactMatch ?z :- ?x skos:exactMatch ?y, ?y skos:exactMatch ?z .

# Collection sub-class (S29): OrderedCollection → Collection
?c rdf:type skos:Collection :- ?c rdf:type skos:OrderedCollection .
```

**Implementation notes**:

- A call to `pg_ripple.load_builtin_rules('skos')` also calls `register_standard_prefixes()` plus registers `("skos", "http://www.w3.org/2004/02/skos/core#")` in `_pg_ripple.prefixes` if absent.
- The `"skos"` rule set is stratifiable: the transitive-closure rules form a single recursive stratum; all other rules are non-recursive.
- Loading `"skos"` after `"rdfs"` or `"owl-rl"` is safe and additive; duplicate rules are absorbed by `ON CONFLICT DO NOTHING`.

#### SKOS-02 — `"skosxl"` Built-in Rule Set (3 rules)

Add a `"skosxl"` branch implementing SKOS-XL label dumb-down chains (S55–S57) so that `skosxl:Label` instances automatically produce plain `skos:prefLabel`, `skos:altLabel`, and `skos:hiddenLabel` triples:

```datalog
# S55: (skosxl:prefLabel, skosxl:literalForm) is a sub-property chain of skos:prefLabel
?x skos:prefLabel   ?l :- ?x skosxl:prefLabel   ?xl, ?xl skosxl:literalForm ?l .

# S56: (skosxl:altLabel, skosxl:literalForm) → skos:altLabel
?x skos:altLabel    ?l :- ?x skosxl:altLabel    ?xl, ?xl skosxl:literalForm ?l .

# S57: (skosxl:hiddenLabel, skosxl:literalForm) → skos:hiddenLabel
?x skos:hiddenLabel ?l :- ?x skosxl:hiddenLabel ?xl, ?xl skosxl:literalForm ?l .
```

The `"skosxl"` prefix `http://www.w3.org/2008/05/skos-xl#` is pre-registered when loading this rule set.

#### SKOS-03 — `"skos-integrity"` SHACL Constraint Rules

Add a `"skos-integrity"` branch that implements the six SKOS integrity conditions as Datalog constraint rules (they fire and produce `_pg_ripple.shacl_violations`-compatible output, or raise PT-coded errors when loaded as hard constraints).

| Rule ID | W3C Statement | Constraint |
|---------|---------------|-----------|
| SKOS-IC-01 | S9 | A resource typed as both `skos:ConceptScheme` and `skos:Concept` violates disjointness |
| SKOS-IC-02 | S13 | A resource with the same literal as both `skos:prefLabel` and `skos:altLabel` (same language) is inconsistent |
| SKOS-IC-03 | S13 | A resource with the same literal as both `skos:prefLabel` and `skos:hiddenLabel` (same language) is inconsistent |
| SKOS-IC-04 | S13 | A resource with the same literal as both `skos:altLabel` and `skos:hiddenLabel` (same language) is inconsistent |
| SKOS-IC-05 | S14 | A resource with two distinct `skos:prefLabel` values sharing the same language tag is inconsistent |
| SKOS-IC-06 | S27 | A pair of concepts linked by both `skos:related` and `skos:broaderTransitive` (inferred) violates disjointness |
| SKOS-IC-07 | S37 | A resource typed as both `skos:Collection` and `skos:Concept` violates disjointness |
| SKOS-IC-08 | S37 | A resource typed as both `skos:Collection` and `skos:ConceptScheme` violates disjointness |
| SKOS-IC-09 | S46 | A pair of concepts linked by both `skos:exactMatch` and `skos:broadMatch` violates disjointness |
| SKOS-IC-10 | S46 | A pair of concepts linked by both `skos:exactMatch` and `skos:relatedMatch` violates disjointness |

These are surfaced via `pg_ripple.validate_skos() RETURNS TABLE(violation_id TEXT, subject TEXT, message TEXT)` — a thin wrapper that calls the constraint rules and formats results.

#### SKOS-05 — SQL Helper Functions

Five new `#[pg_extern]` functions for common thesaurus operations. All functions dictionary-decode results and return plain `TEXT` IRIs.

**`pg_ripple.skos_ancestors(concept_iri TEXT, scheme_iri TEXT DEFAULT '') RETURNS TABLE(ancestor_iri TEXT, depth INT)`**

Returns the transitive closure of `skos:broaderTransitive` for `concept_iri`. When `scheme_iri` is non-empty, restricts results to concepts in that scheme via `skos:inScheme`. Uses `WITH RECURSIVE … CYCLE` internally; depth 0 = the concept itself.

**`pg_ripple.skos_descendants(concept_iri TEXT, scheme_iri TEXT DEFAULT '') RETURNS TABLE(descendant_iri TEXT, depth INT)`**

Returns the `skos:narrowerTransitive` closure. Same parameters as `skos_ancestors`.

**`pg_ripple.skos_label(concept_iri TEXT, lang TEXT DEFAULT 'en') RETURNS TEXT`**

Returns the `skos:prefLabel` of `concept_iri` in the requested language. Falls back to any available `skos:prefLabel` if the requested language is not present. Returns `NULL` if no label exists.

**`pg_ripple.skos_related(concept_iri TEXT) RETURNS TABLE(related_iri TEXT, relation TEXT)`**

Returns all resources linked to `concept_iri` by any `skos:semanticRelation` sub-property. The `relation` column contains the shortened predicate IRI using registered prefixes.

**`pg_ripple.skos_siblings(concept_iri TEXT) RETURNS TABLE(sibling_iri TEXT, shared_broader_iri TEXT)`**

Returns all concepts that share at least one direct `skos:broader` parent with `concept_iri`. The `shared_broader_iri` column contains the common parent.

All five functions are documented in `docs/src/reference/sql-functions.md` and included in the `pg_ripple` schema.

#### RB-01 — Named Bundle API: `load_datalog_bundle()` / `load_shape_bundle()` / `active_datalog_bundles`

**Motivation (riverbank-recommendations item 5)**: riverbank compiler profiles reference rule sets by name (`skos-transitive` in `datalog_rule_bundles`). Without a formal, machine-checkable bundle registry, those references are fragile. The `skos:broaderCycleCheck` SHACL shape also has an explicit dependency on the transitive closure bundle that must be enforced automatically.

**`pg_ripple.load_datalog_bundle(bundle_name TEXT, named_graph TEXT DEFAULT '') RETURNS VOID`**

Activates a named Datalog rule bundle for the given named graph (empty = all graphs). Inserts a row into `_pg_ripple.datalog_bundles` with the current `bundle_version`. Named bundles supported in this release:

| Name | Contents | Activates |
|---|---|---|
| `"skos"` | All 28 W3C SKOS entailment rules (S7–S45) | — |
| `"skos-transitive"` | 7-rule subset: `broaderTransitive` closure, `related` symmetry, `exactMatch` transitivity | — |
| `"skosxl"` | 3 SKOS-XL dumb-down chains (S55–S57) | `"skos"` |
| `"rdfs"` | Existing 13 RDFS rules | — |
| `"owl-rl"` | Existing OWL 2 RL rules | `"rdfs"` |

`load_datalog_bundle` is a superset of `load_builtin_rules` — it calls `load_builtin_rules` internally and additionally records the activation in `_pg_ripple.datalog_bundles`.

**`pg_ripple.load_shape_bundle(bundle_name TEXT) RETURNS VOID`**

Activates a named SHACL shape bundle, resolving and activating Datalog dependencies first. Named shape bundles in this release:

| Name | Shapes | Requires |
|---|---|---|
| `"skos-integrity"` | 10 validators (W3C S9/S13/S14/S27/S37/S46 + ISO 25964-1 structural rules — see SKOS-03 above) | `"skos-transitive"` Datalog bundle |

If the required Datalog bundle is not active, `load_shape_bundle('skos-integrity')` automatically calls `load_datalog_bundle('skos-transitive')` before registering the shapes. The implicit activation is logged at `INFO` level.

**`pg_ripple.active_datalog_bundles`** — a `VIEW` over `_pg_ripple.datalog_bundles`:

```sql
CREATE OR REPLACE VIEW pg_ripple.active_datalog_bundles AS
SELECT bundle_name, bundle_version, loaded_at, named_graph
FROM _pg_ripple.datalog_bundles
ORDER BY bundle_name, named_graph;
```

#### RB-02 — `explain_contradiction()` — Minimal Contradiction Explanation

**Motivation (riverbank-recommendations item 2)**: riverbank v0.7.0 plans `riverbank explain-conflict <iri>`. This algorithm requires access to triples, Datalog inference logs, and RDF-star provenance annotations — all inside pg_ripple. Implementing it in application Python would be a brittle wrapper; it belongs in the database layer.

**`pg_ripple.explain_contradiction(subject_iri TEXT, named_graph TEXT DEFAULT '', max_depth INT DEFAULT 10, mode TEXT DEFAULT 'greedy') RETURNS TABLE(...)`**

Return columns:

| Column | Type | Description |
|---|---|---|
| `element_kind` | `text` | `'triple'`, `'rule'`, or `'assumption'` |
| `subject` | `text` | IRI or blank node |
| `predicate` | `text` | IRI |
| `object` | `text` | IRI or literal |
| `named_graph` | `text` | Graph containing the triple |
| `confidence` | `float4` | `pg:confidence` annotation, if present |
| `rule_name` | `text` | Datalog rule identifier if `element_kind = 'rule'` |
| `contribution` | `text` | Human-readable explanation of why this element contributes to the contradiction |
| `depth` | `int` | Distance from the contradiction root |

**Algorithm**:

1. Run `shacl_validate()` against the subject's graph and collect all `sh:ValidationResult` nodes involving `subject_iri`.
2. For each violation, trace the triple's `prov:wasDerivedFrom` chain backwards through the Datalog inference log to the source triples that triggered it.
3. With `mode = 'greedy'`: compute the minimal hitting set greedily (remove the highest-coverage source triple, repeat until all violations are resolved). With `mode = 'exact'`: enumerate all minimal hitting sets via the AAAI-ATMS algorithm (capped at `max_depth`).
4. Return each element in the minimal set with provenance, confidence, and the triggering rule.

**`pg_ripple.explain_contradiction_json(subject_iri TEXT, named_graph TEXT DEFAULT '', max_depth INT DEFAULT 10, mode TEXT DEFAULT 'greedy') RETURNS JSONB`**

Returns the same data as a structured JSONB document for programmatic consumption.

**Performance target**: ≤ 500 ms on a 100,000-triple graph with a 5-element causal chain in `'greedy'` mode.

pg_regress tests: 6 new tests in `tests/pg_regress/sql/explain_contradiction.sql` — deliberately inconsistent graph, verify result is non-empty and minimal, JSONB output parseable.

#### RB-03 — Federation Trust Layer

**Motivation (riverbank-recommendations item 3)**: riverbank v0.8.0 plans a "remote profile type" pulling SERVICE-federated triples from peer pg_ripple instances. The federation mechanism already exists (v0.16.0/v0.42.0); this deliverable adds the trust-scoring configuration table and source-tagging so foreign triples never silently inherit local confidence scores.

**`pg_ripple.federation_endpoints` table** (created in migration script above).

Usage:

```sql
-- Register a trusted remote endpoint.
INSERT INTO pg_ripple.federation_endpoints (name, endpoint_url, auth_token, min_confidence)
VALUES ('peer-catalogue', 'https://peer.example.org/sparql', '${env:PEER_TOKEN}', 0.6);
```

The `${env:VAR}` secret interpolation convention matches pg-tide's pattern.

**Behaviour changes in the SPARQL engine**:

- When a `SERVICE` clause is executed, pg_ripple looks up the endpoint IRI in `pg_ripple.federation_endpoints`.
- If found: the request proceeds; all triples returned from the remote endpoint are annotated with `pg:sourceTrust = min_confidence` via RDF-star.
- If not found and `pg_ripple.allow_unregistered_service_endpoints = off` (default): raises `PT-SSRF-01` rather than executing the request.
- A registered endpoint that exceeds `timeout_ms` raises a structured error (`PT-FED-TIMEOUT`) rather than hanging the query.

**New GUC**: `pg_ripple.allow_unregistered_service_endpoints` (bool, default `off`).

pg_regress tests: 5 new tests in `tests/pg_regress/sql/federation_trust.sql` — registered endpoint mock, unregistered endpoint rejection, `pg:sourceTrust` annotation verification.

#### RB-04 — `coverage_map()` / `refresh_coverage_map()`

**Motivation (riverbank-recommendations item 4)**: riverbank v0.7.0 plans a daily Prefect flow computing per-topic source density, mean confidence, and violation count as `pgc:CoverageMap` triples. The aggregate queries are standard SQL over named graphs and RDF-star confidence annotations — pure pg_ripple territory. Only the unanswered-question count (joining graph coverage against riverbank's `competency_questions`) stays in the application layer.

**`pg_ripple.coverage_map(named_graphs TEXT[] DEFAULT ARRAY[]::TEXT[], topic_predicate TEXT DEFAULT 'skos:broader', top_k INT DEFAULT 50) RETURNS TABLE(...)`**

When `named_graphs` is empty, scans all non-system named graphs. Groups concepts by their `topic_predicate` cluster (the root concept in that cluster is the `topic_iri`).

Return columns:

| Column | Type | Description |
|---|---|---|
| `topic_iri` | `text` | Topic concept IRI (e.g., a `skos:Concept`) |
| `topic_label` | `text` | `skos:prefLabel` if available |
| `triple_count` | `int` | Total triples in the topic's named subgraph |
| `source_count` | `int` | Distinct `prov:wasDerivedFrom` source IRIs |
| `mean_confidence` | `float4` | Mean `pg:confidence` across all triples in the topic |
| `min_confidence` | `float4` | Minimum confidence (worst fact) |
| `contradiction_count` | `int` | `sh:ValidationResult` violations involving triples in this topic |
| `newest_fact_at` | `timestamptz` | Transaction time of the most recently written triple |
| `oldest_fact_at` | `timestamptz` | Transaction time of the oldest triple |

Topics with zero triples are not included.

**`pg_ripple.refresh_coverage_map(target_graph TEXT, named_graphs TEXT[] DEFAULT ARRAY[]::TEXT[]) RETURNS BIGINT`**

Calls `coverage_map()` internally and writes results as `pgc:CoverageMap` triples into `target_graph`. Returns the number of triples written. Suitable for scheduling as a pg_trickle stream table in `FULL` refresh mode:

```sql
-- Schedule nightly refresh.
SELECT pg_trickle.create_stream(
    name        => 'coverage_refresh',
    query       => $$SELECT pg_ripple.refresh_coverage_map('<coverage>', ARRAY['<trusted>'])$$,
    schedule    => '@daily',
    refresh_mode => 'FULL'
);
```

pg_regress tests: 7 new tests in `tests/pg_regress/sql/coverage_map.sql` — correct aggregation against a known-structure test graph, `pgc:CoverageMap` triples queryable via `sparql()`, zero-triple topics excluded.

### Rule Correctness Notes

The following W3C SKOS statements are **not** modelled as inference rules because they are either integrity conditions (handled by `"skos-integrity"`) or cannot be expressed as monotone Datalog:

- **S13 / S14** (label disjointness / uniqueness per language): These are integrity conditions, not inference rules. Implemented as constraint rules in `"skos-integrity"`.
- **S27** (`skos:related` disjoint with `skos:broaderTransitive`): Integrity condition; detected by comparing inferred `broaderTransitive` triples against asserted `related` triples.
- **S36** (`skos:memberList` items are also `skos:member` values): Requires list traversal over RDF collections (`rdf:first`, `rdf:rest`). Not expressible without built-in list primitives; noted as a known limitation with a tracking issue.
- **S52** (`skosxl:Label` cardinality-1 restriction on `skosxl:literalForm`): Functional-property constraint; equivalent integrity check can be added later.

These known omissions are documented in `docs/src/reference/datalog-built-in-rules.md` under the `"skos"` entry.

### Performance Characteristics

| Operation | Expected cost |
|---|---|
| Load `"skos"` rules (cold) | < 50 ms — `ON CONFLICT DO NOTHING` batch insert |
| First materialisation after load (1 k concepts) | ~200 ms — `WITH RECURSIVE` over small VP tables |
| First materialisation (100 k concepts, 4-level hierarchy) | ~8 s — dominated by `broaderTransitive` closure |
| Subsequent incremental updates (IVM enabled) | ~10 ms per new `skos:broader` triple |
| `skos_ancestors()` call (depth ≤ 10) | < 5 ms — single `WITH RECURSIVE` query |

Materialised `skos:broaderTransitive` triples are stored in the `vp_rare` table (or promoted to a dedicated VP table if the triple count exceeds `pg_ripple.vp_promotion_threshold`). Queries against the transitive closure are therefore index-scans, not live recursion, once the `"skos"` rule set has been materialised.
